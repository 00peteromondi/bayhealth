Privacy

BayAfya privacy notice

BayAfya is designed to keep access proportionate to role, hospital membership, patient approval, and active care context.

1Minimum necessary access
Users should only view the amount of patient information necessary for their active task. Role-aware controls, hospital scope, and patient-specific approvals are used to reduce unnecessary exposure.
2Active encounter and approval paths
Doctors may review fuller patient context during active encounters, including walk-ins and telemedicine sessions. Outside that context, patient detail access requires an approved grant or hospital-authorized path.
3Cross-hospital patient continuity
BayAfya supports one patient identity across facilities to improve continuity of care. Even so, hospital-level access remains controlled and patient detail visibility is not automatically universal.
4Assistant and AI boundaries
BayAfya Assistant uses only the platform context the current user is allowed to access. It should support workflow clarity and structured review, but must not be treated as a substitute for clinical governance or protected-record controls.
5Auditability and monitoring
Operational and clinical actions may be logged to support care continuity, quality review, and security monitoring. Hospitals may review these logs for compliance, patient safety, or incident response.
6Attachments and result records
Lab attachments, scans, PDFs, and other supporting records should be uploaded only where clinically relevant and only viewed by staff with a legitimate role in the patient journey.
